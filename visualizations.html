Dataset

Area, Population and Income of G-20 Countries upto 2013

Source: data.gov.in

Visualizations & Analysis

GDP Over Time (2010-2013)

Key Insights:

  • China: Rapid expansion from ~$6,000B to ~$9,000B (+50%) - steepest growth rate
  • USA: Stable growth from ~$14,500B to ~$16,200B (+11.7%) - maintained largest economy
  • Japan: Economic contraction from ~$5,500B to ~$5,000B (-9%) - post-2011 tsunami impact
  • India: Modest but steady growth from ~$1,600B to ~$2,000B (+25%)

Box Plots: Population, GDP, and GDP per Capita (2013)

Distribution Analysis:

Population

Right-skewed distribution with China and India as extreme outliers (~1.3B each)

GDP

USA ($16.5T) and China ($9T) dominate, with most countries clustering in lower ranges

GDP per Capita

Widest variation (~$10K-42K), revealing vast differences in living standards

GDP vs Population (Top 5 Countries by GDP, 2013)
